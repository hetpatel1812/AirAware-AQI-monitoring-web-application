India Air Quality Overview

Understanding the state of air quality across the nation

🏭 The Air Quality Challenge

India is home to some of the most polluted cities in the world. Rapid urbanization, industrial growth, vehicular emissions, and agricultural practices contribute to deteriorating air quality in many regions. Understanding these patterns is essential for effective mitigation strategies.

🔴

Most Polluted Regions

The Indo-Gangetic Plain, including Delhi-NCR, parts of Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, and Haryana, consistently records the highest pollution levels. Cities like Delhi, Ghaziabad, Noida, and Patna often exceed AQI 300 during winter months.

🟢

Cleanest Regions

Hill stations and coastal cities tend to enjoy better air quality. Shimla, Darjeeling, Ooty, and cities in Kerala and the Northeast generally maintain AQI below 100. Coastal winds and altitude play a significant role in dispersing pollutants.

🍂

Seasonal Patterns

Air quality in India follows strong seasonal trends. Winter (November–February) sees the worst pollution due to temperature inversions and crop burning. Monsoon months (June–September) bring the cleanest air as rains wash away pollutants.

📋

Government Initiatives

The National Clean Air Programme (NCAP) targets a 40% reduction in PM concentrations by 2026. CPCB operates monitoring stations across 344+ cities. The Graded Response Action Plan (GRAP) activates emergency measures when Delhi's AQI spikes.

🌡️ AQI Categories (CPCB Standard)

0 – 50 Good Minimal health impact
51 – 100 Satisfactory Minor breathing discomfort to sensitive people
101 – 200 Moderate Breathing discomfort to people with lung/heart disease
201 – 300 Poor Breathing discomfort on prolonged exposure
301 – 400 Very Poor Respiratory illness on prolonged exposure
401 – 500 Severe Serious health impacts, avoid outdoor activity

🏙️ Major Cities at a Glance

🏛️

Delhi

India's capital frequently tops global pollution charts, especially in winter. AQI often exceeds 400 during November–January due to stubble burning, vehicular emissions, and industrial pollution.

🌊

Mumbai

Mumbai benefits from coastal winds but faces rising pollution from construction, traffic, and industrial activity. Average AQI ranges between 100–200 for most of the year.

🌳

Bengaluru

Known as the Garden City, Bengaluru maintains relatively moderate air quality. However, rapid urbanization and increasing traffic have pushed AQI levels higher in recent years.

🏖️

Chennai

Chennai's coastal location helps maintain better air quality compared to northern cities. Sea breezes help disperse pollutants, keeping AQI mostly in the satisfactory range.
